Project information

  • Category: Wireframe/Graphic Design
  • Client: GamerScoop
  • Project date: JAN, 2022

> YOUR GAMER DREAM TO BUILD A GAMER TEAM.

Gaming news across all platforms and all genres while creating a community for gamers to connect.

Case Study
The Problem

Users need an app for everything gaming news and new game/console release as they sometimes miss the window of release, and also have a gaming community to belong to and feel accepted.

Solution

Create an app that focuses on gaming news across all platforms and all genres while creating a community for gamers to connect

Project

Non Profit Redesign

Roles

User Research/WireFrame Designer

Time

6 Week Project

Interview Plan/Interview Transcript

For our interviews, we sought to discover the preferred gaming method, thoughts, and opinions based on the gaming scene and the chances of making friends online.

In this research project, we planned and conducted three interviews with potential users of ages 24-46.

Affinity Diagram

We used an affinity diagram to organize many ideas about the gaming scene, video games, and gaming communities into their natural relationships. The organized output from a brainstorming session led us to several ideas on how to proceed with Gamerscoop. Using the affinity diagram to generate, organize, and consolidate information related to Gamerscoop. After we developed ideas, we grouped them according to their affinity or similarity.

User Persona

Our user Cassandra was used to have a better understanding and empathy with the end-users and to Gain a perspective to help us have a deeper understanding of potential users of gamerscoop to join a safe gaming community.

Define And Ideate
User Insight

Cassandra is a 28 yr RNA nurse looking for a new game to play with her friends. She's seeking a competitive game where she can challenge herself to reach the top game ranks. Cassandra wants a nontoxic gaming community to meet like-minded people and find casual gaming. She wants to balance her gaming work life.

Final Thoughts/Conclusion

We have to have more information and make our wireframes easier to read and more user-friendly. Have more information on the slide and treat the presentation as a story next time. Have a closing slide to close out the presentation and have answers to questions ready. Be more informative with wireframes walkthrough and be more open with fixing clickable buttons and fixing lag regarding moving through the app.
